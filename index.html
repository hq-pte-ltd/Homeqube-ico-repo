What is homeqube?

Homeqube is a home-building application powered by artificial intelligence (AI) that empowers the user to visualize his future home. Through its
built-in network of manufacturers, suppliers, contractors, architects, and engineers, Homeqube helps future homeowners optimize their design
according to every budget, taste, lifestyle, and more.

When using the app, Homeqube will guide you through the entire process of designing your home. This includes automatically plotting your lot
area through optical character recognition (OCR), designing your home using our “user-centric robust controls” (knobs), and eventually generating
essential documents you would need to build your house before move-in.

What makes the home design experience on Homeqube different is that anyone with access to the Internet can use it, whether or not you are in
the actual process of buying or building your own home. Information on cost, mobility, and dynamics is readily available. This gives users the
power to make informed decisions when it comes to life-changing commitments such as having a roof over their heads.

What’s more, users are incentivized to use the application through QUBE tokens and gamification. The more they engage, the more data will be
analyzed on the activities that provide the most value to all users. This will help the app grow, by creating more features that result in higher
engagement.

A decentralized online platform for home design such as Homeqube has never been done before because it also comes at the heels of global
problems that the world has never experienced before: a once-in-a-lifetime pandemic, the worsening and accelerating impacts of climate change,
and heightened cyber insecurity. Through this application, we envision a significant reduction in common architectural and engineering
inefficiencies such as material and labor waste while giving both the supplier and end-user access to useful information and resources in the
home building process.

Homeqube is being developed by a team of experts from multiple disciplines with years of experience in architecture, engineering,
manufacturing, and construction. One of our premises is that the main population of built architecture was built on top-down construction
processes, and therefore the aggregated aesthetic potentialities of thereof are limited and based thus. We want to introduce a new batch of
potential aesthetics for the next century living with AI in our midst.

Our mission is to make the home building processes accessible to all through AI and blockchain technologies to serve humanity’s aggregated
home requirements for long-term sustained success. Our vision is to provide a better Home Building Industry Environment Ecosystem through
our ‘socio- techno-economy” platforms that will create more justice, equity, liberty, for posterity.

Core Values: Above Board. Corporate Transparency. Love for God and Neighbor. Home-building For Everyone.

Home Building App

At the heart of the app are the microsystems to build the houses. The microsystems are based on the deconstruction philosophy of the app, where different combinations of designs can be produced
from the basic parts of the structure. These microsystems are operated using knobs to modify the design of the house, based on the user’s preferences. As you turn the knobs, you will see real-time
implications in the cost of your home, lifestyle metrics, mobility metrics, and others values to guide you in the design. As the app’s name implies, the massing of the home is based on cubes.

The freemium version of the app will have the first set of knobs, which can produce cube massing. The arrangement of the cubes will depend on the three knobs, namely the generation path knob,
density knob, and complexity knob. Users can have various arrangement combinations of the cubes, which are constrained within the allowable buildable area and a maximum number of storie
indicated in the input.

Long lead tIme

The traditional process of constructing homes can be long, painful, and costly. The first step is to hire professionals to help you conceptualize and design your house. The next step is to apply for permits from a building official. Only upon its issuance can construction commence. The process for a typical two-storey home takes several months to complete without factoring in unforeseen events. Homeqube can shorten lead time by using artificial intelligence, prefabrication, and easier parts assembly.

Lack of public information in Architecture and Engineering

Designing and building homes require technical knowledge in architecture, engineering, and construction. Obtaining this knowledge requires studying for years, working with professionals, and gathering licensure for professional practices. This traditional process limits the knowledge of building homes.
In Homeqube, we introduce our way of designing and building your homes. We devised a platform that simplifies the design building process and can be easily absorbed by the users.

No direct connection to manufacturers in the design process

Construction of homes includes reaching out to suppliers, canvassing, and purchasing. These processes take time, and yet sometimes, the materials that arrive at the site are still either wrong or incomplete.

Homeqube will have a feature that connects the parts of your home to a recommended manufacturer. This shortens the canvassing process and helps to ensure that the materials requested are of good quality.

No linkage between e-commerce and house design

Home furnishing company IKEA enables its users to browse and canvas products without being physically present at their warehouse through their online store. Recently, SM supermalls also released an online platform for shopping online.

Similar to that model, the Homeqube app provides its users with information on specifications, brands, and cost of home construction parts and materials.

High Carbon Emissions from Construction Materials

Steel and concrete are fundamental materials used in construction, but their production results in 4% of the global CO2 emissions. This greenhouse contributes to the increased rates of climate change, making them not environmentally friendly.

Homeqube parts designs use special composites, which are alternative structural materials to concrete and steel. Our calculation shows how these materials can lessen energy consumption and carbon emissions, thus making them eco-friendly.

Inefficiencies of Skilled Labor, especially on Pandemic Scenarios

Homeqube parts have low skilled assembly requirements since their parts are dry and pre- engineered. Tedious design computations and quality requirements of the previous eras won’t be relevant. Building the parts from the Homeqube does not require deep technical and construction skills to understand. The plans, assembly methodologies, and other documents will ensure the smooth erection of your home.

Low Resilience of Residential Structures against Strong Winds and Earthquake

Poor quality houses are the usual targets of severe weather and earthquake. The typhoon Odette, the strongest typhoon to hit the Philippines in 2021, damaged around 597,000 houses. While we can’t do anything about the inevitable severer weather, our company advocates for strong and resilient houses. The parts and assembly methods of Homeqube are proven and tested to withstand strong winds from up to 310kph, and earthquakes from up to 8.1 magnitudes.

Artificial intelligence and machine learning

Before the popularization of machine learning, the computational design
approach is used in making design models. This approach is based on
running algorithms and codes to make a parametric family representation
of shapes and sizes. With the introduction of machine learning and AI,
optimization and exploration of different designs were added. This
additional step in the design process gives users different possible design
combinations and comes up with the most optimal design.

Here at Homeqube, we’ll harness the power of AI and ML to make
different possible combinations of home designs, and provide the most
optimal design based on the user’s need. Also, as the system generates
more data, more home design styles will the unearthed. Our system will
push both the human and machine capacity in generating creative ideas.
