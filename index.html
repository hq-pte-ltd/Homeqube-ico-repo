Homeqube is a home-building application powered by artificial
intelligence (AI)that empowers the user to visualize his future
home.Through its built-in network of manufacturers, suppliers,
contractors, architects, and engineers, Homeqube helps future
homeowners optimize their design according to every budget,
taste, and lifestyle.

Home Building App

At the heart of the app are the microsystems to build the houses. The
microsystems are based on the deconstruction philosophy of the app,
where different combinations of designs can be produced from the basic
parts of the structure. These microsystems are operated using knobs to
modify the design of the house, based on the user’s preferences. As you
turn the knobs, you will see real-time implications in the cost of your
home, lifestyle metrics, mobility metrics, and others values to guide you in
the design. As the app’s name implies, the massing of the home is based
on cubes.

The freemium version of the app will have the first set of knobs, which can
produce cube massing. The arrangement of the cubes will depend on the
three knobs, namely the generation path knob, density knob,
and complexity knob. Users can have various arrangement combinations of the
cubes, which are constrained within the allowable buildable area and a
maximum number of stories indicated in the input.

confronting the
homebuilding

Long lead tIme

The traditional process of constructing homes can be long, painful, and costly. The first step is to hire professionals to help you conceptualize and design your house. The next step is to apply for permits from a building official. Only upon its issuance can construction commence. The process for a typical two-storey home takes several months to complete without factoring in unforeseen events. Homeqube can shorten lead time by using artificial intelligence, prefabrication, and easier parts assembly.

Lack of public information on architecture

Architecture is one of the most difficult courses among all other courses. Aside from the exams, plates, thesis defence, and other college works, they need also at least two years of work experience before taking a licensure exam. The course is also expensive due to the learning materials, scale model projects, and other instruments used by architects. Combining these two aspects, obtaining the skills and knowledge in architecture is difficult.

No direct connection to manufacturers in the design process

Construction of homes includes reaching out to suppliers, canvassing, and purchasing. These processes take time, and yet sometimes, the materials that arrive at the site are still either wrong or incomplete.

Homeqube will have a feature that connects the parts of your home to a recommended manufacturer. This shortens the canvassing process and helps to ensure that the materials requested are of good quality.

No linkage between e-commerce and house design

Home furnishing company IKEA enables its users to browse and canvas products without being physically present at their warehouse through their online store. Recently, SM supermalls also released an online platform for shopping online.

Similar to that model, the Homeqube app provides its users with information on specifications, brands, and cost of home construction parts and materials.

High Carbon Emissions from Construction Materials

Steel and concrete are fundamental materials used in construction, but their production results in 4% of the global CO2 emissions. This greenhouse contributes to the increased rates of climate change, making them not environmentally friendly.

Homeqube parts designs use special composites, which are alternative structural materials to concrete and steel. Our calculation shows how these materials can lessen energy consumption and carbon emissions, thus making them eco-friendly.

Inefficiencies of Skilled Labor, especially on Pandemic Scenarios

Homeqube parts have low skilled assembly requirements since their parts are dry and pre- engineered. Tedious design computations and quality requirements of the previous eras won’t be relevant. Building the parts from the Homeqube does not require deep technical and construction skills to understand. The plans, assembly methodologies, and other documents will ensure the smooth erection of your home.

Low Resilience of Residential Structures against Strong Winds and Earthquake

Poor quality houses are the usual targets of severe weather and earthquake. The typhoon Odette, the strongest typhoon to hit the Philippines in 2021, damaged around 597,000 houses. While we can’t do anything about the inevitable severer weather, our company advocates for strong and resilient houses. The parts and assembly methods of Homeqube are proven and tested to withstand strong winds from up to 310kph, and earthquakes from up to 8.1 magnitudes.

Aritificial intelligence and machine learning

Before the popularization of machine learning, the computational design
approach is used in making design models. This approach is based on
running algorithms and codes to make a parametric family representation
of shapes and sizes. With the introduction of machine learning and AI,
optimization and exploration of different designs were added. This
additional step in the design process gives users different possible design
combinations and comes up with the most optimal design.

Here at Homeqube, we’ll harness the power of AI and ML to make
different possible combinations of home designs, and provide the most
optimal design based on the user’s need. Also, as the system generates
more data, more home design styles will the unearthed. Our system will
push both the human and machine capacity in generating creative ideas.
