What Are Carbon Credits?

Greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide and methane are emitted on a large scale in everyday life, across nearly all aspects of it, and are widely linked to worsening global warming and climate change.

Human activities -- from what we eat to electricity to the house we live in -- involve greenhouse gas release into the atmosphere.

Carbon footprint is the annual equivalent amount of GHGs released based on these activities. It’s imperative that we start reducing this footprint to avert an impending environmental catastrophe.

What are carbon credits?
Carbon credits, carbon offsets, or technically referred to as Certified Emission Reduction (CER) credits, are simplified units used to quantify either the removal or prevention of 1 ton of CO2-equivalent substances from being released to the atmosphere.

This was pioneered by the Clean Development Mechanism (CDM) of the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC) as a global mechanism to involve governments and the private sector to take an active role in reducing anthropogenic Greenhouse Gas (GHG) emissions.

What are carbon credits for?
Carbon credits represent a buy-and sell mechanism that financially rewards efforts to reduce harmful GHG emissions, in turn yielding benefits for our environment.

In the second half of 2021, the weighted average price of 1 CER credit is USD 4.73, which fluctuates depending on the type and area of project, as well as demand for the credits (Ecosystem Marketplace, 2021). With tightening global regulations for carbon emissions as well as developing carbon markets in other countries, it is projected that the price of CER credits can increase 50-fold within the next 30 years according to BloombergNEF

The carbon credit market is composed of compulsory and voluntary credits. Voluntary carbon credits are increasingly being bought by private corporations as a means to effectively offset their carbon footprint. These carbon credits are often generated by green technology projects such as renewable energy plants, reforestation projects, and other innovative carbon-reduced alternative processes that benefit the environment in the long term.
