Design and build your home using homeqube

For users who want to build their homes in the Homeqube app, this is the recommended path.

At the heart of the app are the microsystems to build the houses. The microsystems are based on the deconstruction philosophy of the app,
where different combinations of designs can be produced from the basic parts of the structure. These microsystems are operated using knobs to
modify the design of the house, based on the user’s preferences. As you turn the knobs, you will see real-time implications in the cost of your
home, lifestyle metrics, mobility metrics, and others values to guide you in the design. As the app’s name implies, the massing of the home is
based on cubes.

The freemium version of the app will have the first set of knobs, which can produce cube massing. The arrangement of the cubes will depend on
the three knobs, namely the generation path knob, density knob, and complexity knob. Users can have various arrangement combinations of the
cubes, which are constrained within the allowable buildable area and a maximum number of stories indicated in the input.

As the app development progress, more knobs will be further developed. These knobs will represent algorithmic processes and design variables
on different styles of massing, parts, mobility, design intent, enclosures, and other variables that affect home design. These knobs will expand
the possibilities of home design by tweaking these variables that affect the design of homes. By cloud computing, it is possible to generate a
Tradespace plot on the different pegs of houses in a few days.

Become a Smart Contractor and Harness the Power of Smart Contracting

A traditional contractor for residential houses is bound to follow the agreed contract to receive
payments. Numerous forms of payments, including progress billing and retention billings, are verified
by a third party. The assigned third party will check if the constructions follow the technical
specifications, within time, and budget. Also, they check legal requirements to assure that the
structure is following building code standards. These construction practices are prone to the long wait
time for contractors to have their billing, as they are fully reliant on the third parties’ validation.
